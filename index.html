About Me

I’m an undergraduate studying computer science in the College of Engineering at Cornell University. I enjoy math and physics and spend my free time flying drones, taking pictures, and throwing pottery. I’m part of the computer science team of Cornell’s Aerial Robotics club which competes in the annual International Aerial Robotics Competition. Additionally, I work with Misha Padidar as a research assistant in Cornell’s Applied Math department optimizing the coil shape for stellarator fusion reactors.

Evolutionary algorithm assisted antenna design

I wrote an evolutionary algorithm in Python to optimize the antenna shape for communications antennas, the final design is to be used on F-5 Navy training jets. The plate antenna operates from 108-400MHz, the evolutionary algorithm designed antenna is about twice as good as the old design and exceeds the specification by 25%. Stellerator Research Assistant

I am currently working under Misha Padidar in Cornell's Applied Math Department on optimizing the magnetic confinement coils’ geometry for stellarator fusion reactors. The process involves finding optimal magnetic surfaces to confine the plasma and the coil shapes that make those fields. I’ve built optimizers using gradient descent, Gauss-Newton, linear and logistic regression, and LaGrange multipliers.

Tribology Reseach Assistant

As part of the tribology lab in the Unversity of Nevada, Reno’s Mechanical Engineering department, I helped automate data-analysis and the production of ionic liquids. I wrote code that processed wear-track depth data to determine characteristics like wear volume and surface roughness. Additionally, I helped in the effort to use machine learning to extrapolate data beyond the capabilities of the testing equipment. Finally, I helped design and develop an Arduino controlled synthesizer of ionic liquid. I coded an image processing algorithm to determine the layer boundary between two liquids, this helped automate the refinement of ionic liquid.
